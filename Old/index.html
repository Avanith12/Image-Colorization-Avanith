About Our Project

Problem

Grayscale images, while historically significant, often fail to convey the full emotional and visual richness that color provides. Manually adding color is a highly meticulous process, requiring skilled artists, a deep understanding of shading, and significant time investment. This makes large-scale restoration projects difficult and expensive.

Solution

To address this, we designed an AI-powered deep learning model combining a ResNet18 encoder and a Dynamic UNet decoder within a Conditional GAN (cGAN) framework. Leveraging both the power of convolutional neural networks and adversarial learning, our model can automatically predict and restore realistic colors to grayscale images ‚Äî offering fast, high-quality results without manual intervention.

Real-world Use Cases

  • Restoring Old Memories: Revive cherished family photographs that have faded over decades, bringing loved ones back to life in vivid color.
  • Colorizing Historical Archives: Breathe new life into black-and-white photographs from wars, cultural events, and historical milestones, making them more relatable for modern audiences.
  • Creative Enhancements: Empower artists, content creators, and photographers to creatively reinterpret grayscale images, adding depth and emotional resonance through colorization.
  • Film and Media Restoration: Update classic films, documentaries, and vintage media by transforming black-and-white scenes into full-color visuals without losing authenticity.
  • Educational Tools: Help educators illustrate historical concepts vividly by converting black-and-white teaching materials into colorful, engaging content.

Demo & Model Information

Model Overview

Our model architecture consists of a ResNet18 encoder combined with a Dynamic UNet decoder. It is trained within a Conditional Generative Adversarial Network (cGAN) framework, using a PatchGAN (70x70) discriminator. The generator is optimized with a combination of L1 loss (for pixel-level accuracy) and GAN loss (for realism and sharpness).

Training Information

  • Dataset: Subset of ImageNet (~10,000 images)
  • Tools & Libraries: PyTorch, FastAI, OpenCV, NumPy
  • Training Time: Approximately 10 hours
  • Image Size: 256x256 pixels

Evaluation Metrics

The model's performance was evaluated using Mean Squared Error (MSE), Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM), and qualitative visual comparisons to ensure the generated colorizations are both accurate and aesthetically pleasing.

Future Improvements

Moving forward, we plan to enhance our project with several key improvements:

  • Real-time colorization support for instantaneous results.
  • Optimization for mobile devices and web-based applications.
  • Interactive controls to adjust color vibrancy and warmth manually.
  • Use of Vision Transformers and Diffusion Models for higher realism.
  • Fine-tuning the model on specific domains (e.g., portraits, landscapes, vintage photos).
  • Enhancing robustness to very low-quality or heavily degraded input images.
